Technical Report

Introduction

H I was assigned the task of developing an application tailored for a primary school environment. Embarking on this journey, my initial focus was crafting a user-friendly and secure login page. In the realm of XML, I meticulously designed this page, adhering to a philosophy of simplicity. Within its confines, users encountered the familiar fields of email and password, accompanied by two pivotal buttons—login and sign-up. The intricacies of this login page were brought to life through Kotlin, where I orchestrated the necessary coding to ensure its seamless functionality. The synergy between XML and Kotlin paved the way for an intuitive user experience, setting the foundation for a cohesive application. Transitioning seamlessly into the sign-up page, I continued my design philosophy, employing XML to accommodate five distinct text fields. These fields served as conduits for acquiring essential user information required for a successful login. Augmented by two strategically placed buttons—sign-up and back—the page enabled users to navigate seamlessly through the registration process. In Kotlin, my coding endeavours extended beyond mere button functionalities. I implemented logic to scrutinise user input, ensuring that the password and confirm password fields harmonised. A critical safeguard against mismatched entries was instituted, preventing erroneous data from being stored in the application's archives.
